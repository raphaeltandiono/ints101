Analysis Plan

Quantitative Analysis: Regression Modeling

Quantitative analysis will utilize panel data regression models to investigate the relationship between VC syndication network structures and ecosystem resilience, particularly during downturns. Resilience indicators (e.g., funding stability, recovery speed) will serve as dependent variables. Key independent variables will include network structure metrics (e.g., density, centrality measures) and their changes during crisis periods, alongside control variables. Interaction terms will be employed specifically to test how network structure moderates the impact of downturns (testing H1-H3).

Model Specification (Illustrative for H1):

To test H1 (fragmentation impact on recovery), a potential model specification is:

RecoverySpeede,t = β0 + β1(ΔDensitye,crisis) + β2(DownturnSeveritye,t) + Controlse,t + εe,t

Where:
RecoverySpeede,t = Time for funding in ecosystem 'e' to return to trend after downturn 't'.
ΔDensitye,crisis = Change in network density for ecosystem 'e' during the crisis period (a measure of fragmentation; negative value indicates decreased density/increased fragmentation).
DownturnSeveritye,t = Measure of the economic shock's intensity.
Controlse,t = Vector of control variables (e.g., ecosystem size, industry composition, pre-crisis density).
H1 predicts β1 > 0 (a larger *decrease* in density, i.e., more negative ΔDensity, leads to slower recovery, meaning a positive coefficient relates higher density change to faster recovery).

Addressing Endogeneity:

To address potential endogeneity issues like reverse causality or omitted variables, lagged independent variables (e.g., using pre-crisis network structure or change in density from the *start* of the crisis) will be prioritized. Instrumental variable (IV) techniques will be considered if appropriate instruments related to network changes but not directly to resilience outcomes can be identified.

Hypothesis Testing (H1 Focus):

Hypothesis 1 posits that greater network fragmentation (decreased density) during downturns hinders ecosystem resilience (slower recovery). This will be tested by examining the sign, magnitude, and statistical significance of the coefficient β1 associated with the change in network density (ΔDensity) during the crisis. A statistically significant positive coefficient for β1 (as defined in the model above) would provide support for H1, indicating that ecosystems maintaining higher density (less fragmentation) exhibit faster funding recovery.

Qualitative Analysis: Thematic Analysis & Triangulation

Crucially, qualitative thematic analysis of interviews and potential case study data will complement the quantitative findings. This phase explores the underlying mechanisms, contextual factors, and the 'why' behind observed statistical relationships, thereby strengthening causal inference through triangulation.

Step 1: Coding Qualitative Data

Transcripts and notes will be coded thematically, focusing on experiences related to network changes during crises, syndication behavior shifts, information flow, resource access, and perceived impacts on portfolio companies and overall ecosystem function.

Step 2: Identifying Mechanisms (H1 Focus)

Analysis will specifically seek to understand *why* fragmentation might hinder recovery (supporting H1). Themes explored may include reduced access to diverse information, difficulties in coordinating support for startups, breakdown of trust in wider networks, increased search costs for partners, or flight to 'safer' core partners, potentially stranding peripheral actors.

Step 3: Triangulation

Qualitative insights regarding the consequences of network fragmentation (or stability) will be compared with the quantitative results (e.g., the significance of β1 in the H1 model). Congruence between findings will strengthen the interpretation, while divergence may highlight contextual factors or alternative explanations requiring further investigation.
